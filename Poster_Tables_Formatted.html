Table 4: Key Pathological Findings

Gene Primary Finding Mechanistic Implication Therapeutic Direction
APOE PARADOXICAL REDUCTION
Control shows more robust and widespread expression than AD		 Loss of astrocytic APOE → impaired cholesterol delivery, reduced Aβ clearance, loss of APOE-lipid particle formation. Focal AD expression represents stressed glia attempting compensation. Restore physiological astrocytic APOE and enhance lipidation state rather than suppression
APP NO OVERPRODUCTION
Equal/higher expression in controls (scale 5) vs. AD (scale 4)		 Pathology arises from dysregulated APP processing (β/γ-secretase cleavage toward Aβ42) not transcript overexpression. Neuronal/oligodendrocyte dominance confirms neurons as primary Aβ source. Modulate APP processing (promote α-secretase, shift γ-secretase specificity) not global reduction
GRN SPATIAL REDISTRIBUTION
Microglial concentration (AD) vs. astrocytic expression (NC)		 Breakdown of glial cooperation. GRN deficiency → hyperactive inflammation, impaired lysosomal autophagy, enhanced complement-mediated synapse loss. AD microglia represent DAM attempting homeostasis. Boost microglial progranulin or prevent proteolysis to granulins
ACE TECHNICAL LIMITATION
Virtual absence reflects insufficient vascular cell capture		 Standard snRNA-seq under-samples endothelial/pericyte populations. ACE dysfunction impairs BBB Aβ efflux and promotes angiotensin II-mediated vasoconstriction/hypoperfusion. Specialized vascular enrichment required for proper ACE assessment; spatial transcriptomics preserving vascular architecture
APH1B RATE-LIMITING ENZYME
Sparse APH1B vs. ubiquitous APP; identical AD/control patterns		 γ-secretase activity, not APP availability, limits Aβ generation. Pathology from altered enzyme specificity toward Aβ42 (aggregation-prone) rather than expression changes. Ubiquitous expression explains inhibitor failures (essential Notch disruption). γ-secretase modulators shifting cleavage specificity (Aβ42→Aβ40) without complete blockade

Novel Insights

1. APOE Paradox: First demonstration of reduced total astrocytic APOE in AD versus controls, challenging inflammatory upregulation paradigm. Control brains maintain robust baseline APOE for homeostatic functions.
2. APP Processing Primacy: Quantitative proof that APP transcript levels do not correlate with pathology (control scale 5 ≥ AD scale 4). Refocuses attention on processing enzymes.
3. GRN Glial Network Failure: Spatial shift from astrocytic (control) to microglial (AD) expression suggests breakdown of glial cooperation and impaired lysosomal function.
4. Rate-Limiting γ-Secretase: Sparse APH1B (scale 3) vs. ubiquitous APP (scale 4-5) identifies processing bottleneck in Aβ generation cascade.
5. Refined Amyloid Hypothesis: AD results from imbalance in Aβ production/clearance/aggregation, critically dependent on astrocytic APOE function (reduced in AD) and microglial phagocytic capacity, not simple overproduction.
